Why Safety Matters

Scuba diving is exciting, but it comes with risks. Following safety guidelines ensures that you and your dive partners can enjoy the underwater world without danger.

Essential Safety Tips

  • Always dive with a buddy.
  • Check your equipment before every dive.
  • Plan your dive and stick to the plan.
  • Ascend slowly to avoid decompression sickness.
  • Never hold your breath while diving.
  • Stay within your training and certification limits.
  • Be aware of your air supply at all times.

Emergency Preparedness

Divers should be trained in emergency procedures, such as sharing air, making a controlled emergency ascent, and recognizing the signs of decompression sickness.
